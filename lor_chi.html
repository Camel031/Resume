Letter of Recommendation — Yi-Cian Huang (黃以謙)

March 2026

To the Selection Committee,
Academia Sinica Research Internship Program:

I am writing to recommend Yi-Cian Huang (黃以謙) for your research internship program. I am an Associate Professor at the Department of Computer Science and Information Engineering, National Taiwan Normal University, where I lead the Applied Cryptography and Information Security Laboratory. Yi-Cian has taken four of my courses over the past two years and is currently developing an independent research proposal in my laboratory.

The clearest evidence of his engineering ability came from my Software Engineering course. Yi-Cian served as full-stack Tech Lead and Sandbox subteam lead on Normal OJ, a production online judge system inherited from previous cohorts. He was responsible for bringing the existing codebase to a stable baseline, then leading a sustained effort to extend its architecture — spanning the sandbox execution layer, backend API, frontend, and cloud deployment on GCP and Cloudflare. What impressed me was his instinct for the parts of the work that tend to be neglected: he kept the CI/CD pipeline clean, coordinated architecture decisions across sub-teams, and when the project manager was absent, stepped in to run meetings without being asked. His end-of-semester reflection was one of the more thoughtful I have received: he wrote about team dynamics, task decomposition, and the tradeoffs in maintaining an inherited codebase with a clarity I do not typically see at the undergraduate level.

His work in the Advanced Information Security Practices for Industries: Attack and Defense course showed a different but equally important quality. The course places students in adversarial red/blue team scenarios, and I was impressed by how Yi-Cian approached it: rather than treating offense and defense as two separate assignments, he used each side to sharpen his understanding of the other — building an attack while simultaneously reasoning about what a defender would need to catch it. That is the disposition I look for, and not many students at his level show it.

At the end of that semester, Yi-Cian reached out to ask about joining the lab. His message was specific: he named fully homomorphic encryption, zero-knowledge proofs, and LLM security as areas he had been reading about independently. That kind of initiative is not common. The research question he subsequently developed — using FHE to enable privacy-preserving behavioral authentication, anchored to the user's hardware authenticator as the trust root — reflects genuine independent thinking. He identified a gap the security community has largely accepted as a given, and proposed a non-trivial design to address it. The project is in its early phase, but the quality of his reasoning gives me real confidence in his potential as a researcher.

I recommend Yi-Cian without reservation and am happy to discuss his candidacy further.

Yours sincerely,
Chi Po-Wen (紀博文)
